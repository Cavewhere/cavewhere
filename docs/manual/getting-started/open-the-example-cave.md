# Open the Example Cave

## Why / when you need this

A fresh install opens on an empty cave, which is a hard place to learn from —
there is nothing to orbit, no trips to open, no notes to look at. Before you have
surveyed anything of your own, the fastest way to see what CaveWhere does is to
open a cave someone has already built.

**Phake Cave 3000** is that cave. It is a complete, multi-trip example project —
survey data, notes, and scraps morphed onto a 3D model — and it is the cave you
see in this manual's screenshots. Opening it gives you real data to explore while
you read the rest of Getting Started.

![A 3D view of Phake Cave 3000: hand-drawn passage sketches morphed and draped as textured carpets over the red survey line, its two levels and the vertical connection between them visible in three dimensions.](../images/scraps-carpet-orbit-poster.png)
*Phake Cave 3000, the example cave — a finished project you can orbit and pick
apart once you've downloaded it.*

## On macOS and Windows: click to open

On macOS and Windows, one click downloads the example cave and opens it:

**[▶ Open the Example Cave](https://cavewhere.com/open?repo=https://github.com/Cavewhere/PhakeCave3000.git)**

Your browser hands the link to CaveWhere, which pops up the **Clone Repository**
dialog described below. The link works anywhere it appears — this page, an email,
a web page — so you can share it with anyone who has CaveWhere installed.

## On Linux: paste the link into the app

On Linux, clicking the link doesn't open CaveWhere, so you hand the cave to it
yourself:

1. In CaveWhere, open **File → Open from Link…** (the **File** menu is a button at
   the top of the sidebar on Linux).
2. Paste the repository URL:

   ```
   https://github.com/Cavewhere/PhakeCave3000.git
   ```
3. Click **Open**. CaveWhere opens the same **Clone Repository** dialog.

## Download and open

However you got here, CaveWhere shows a **Clone Repository** dialog naming the
repository (`github.com / Cavewhere/PhakeCave3000`) with a **Destination** folder
below it. The destination is where the downloaded cave will live on your disk —
click the path to choose a different folder, or leave it at the default.

The example repository is public, so **you don't need to sign in** — leave the
**Account** picker alone. (Signing in is only for private team repositories, which
the Collaboration chapter covers.)

Click **Clone & Open**. A progress bar tracks the download, and when it finishes
CaveWhere opens Phake Cave 3000. Because CaveWhere
[holds one project at a time](../projects-and-files/open-a-project.md), it first
asks what to do with whatever project is currently open, exactly as **Open** does.

The cave arrives as a normal project on your disk — one you can orbit, edit, and
experiment with freely. Your changes stay on your machine; they don't reach the
shared copy online unless you deliberately sync, so the example is a safe place to
try things out.
