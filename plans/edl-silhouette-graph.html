EDL silhouette transfer functions

Per-neighbor darkening vs the log-depth gap g = |Δlog2(linearDepthFromNear)|. Final pixel = exp(−Σ darkening / 8) over the 8 neighbors.

cloud↔cloud (far-side shape): min(g·strength, maxDarken) cloud↔opaque (border): mix(base, maxDarken, clamp(g·silhStrength,0,1))
= baseline (1500) ÷ log-depth-span normalizer. Drives cloud↔cloud.

How to read it. The x-axis is the log-depth gap g; the second tick row shows the matching physical depth ratio 2^g (e.g. g=0.1 ≈ neighbor 7% farther, g=1.0 = 2× farther). The y-axis is per-neighbor darkening fed into exp(−Σ/8) — higher = darker.

Why they don't line up. cloud↔cloud (yellow) has no floor and a slope of strength (~300), so it slams into maxDarken by g≈ — a near depth-step is already full black. cloud↔opaque (blue) starts at the floor and rises with slope silhStrength (~3), reaching maxDarken only at g≈. To make them coincide over a depth range, the two slopes (and the floor) have to be reconciled — e.g. drop strength toward silhStrength·(maxDarken−base) and/or raise the floor. Match the curves here, then read the values into the in-app sliders.

Depth-position independence. Because both operate on log2 of linear depth, a given ratio of neighbor depths gives the same darkening anywhere in the scene — but a fixed physical step (say 0.5 m) is a large ratio near the camera and a tiny one far away, so the same wall reads stronger up close. That's the log axis doing its job.
